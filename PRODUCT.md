# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MovU serves Taylor's University students and staff who need trusted campus carpooling, drivers who can offer verified shared rides, and administrators who monitor safety, verification, payment-readiness operations, and reports. Riders and drivers use the system in a mobile-first context, while admins use a multi-page operations dashboard for repeated review and response work.

## Product Purpose

MovU is a verified campus carpooling platform for matching riders and drivers inside the Taylor's University community. Success means a rider can verify email and request a ride with real route coordinates, a verified driver can post and manage trips, the system can recommend route-aware matches, live trip safety can be monitored, and admins can resolve verification, SOS, report, and audit workflows with clear accountability. Payment collection is disabled until an approved provider is configured.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, energetic, and practical. The product should feel like a real campus mobility tool inspired by Grab, Bolt, and DiDi: polished, operationally clear, and fast to understand, without looking like a generic AI-generated dashboard.

## Anti-references

Do not collapse every feature into one overloaded page. Avoid decorative AI-purple gradients, generic glass panels, identical card grids, vague startup copy, fake metrics, and marketing-page hero patterns in authenticated admin surfaces. Avoid visuals that make MovU look like a public ride-hailing clone rather than a verified campus carpooling service.

## Design Principles

1. Separate workflows by job: verification, trip operations, safety, payment readiness, and reports should live in focused pages.
2. Make trust visible: roles, verification status, bans, vehicle approval, SOS state, and report history should be easy to scan.
3. Prioritize operational clarity: admins should be able to act quickly without hunting for controls.
4. Keep campus context explicit: language, labels, sample data, and flows should feel grounded in Taylor's University.
5. Design for production credibility: every screen should reflect real operational states and deployable workflows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible controls. Support English, Simplified Chinese, and Bahasa Malaysia across admin UI text. Respect reduced motion preferences and avoid color-only status communication, especially for verification, SOS, payment, and safety states.
